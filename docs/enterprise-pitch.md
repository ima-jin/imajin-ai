# You're Already Paying for This Problem

Your organization runs on human intelligence. The knowledge in your people's heads — who to call, what was decided, how things actually work, what the client really wants.

And you're spending a fortune trying to access it.

Jira tells you what's assigned. Not what's understood. Salesforce logs the calls. Not the relationships. Confluence stores the docs. Nobody reads them. The standup exists because the only way to know what's happening is to ask everyone, out loud, every morning.

You're not running a company. You're running a context synchronization ritual.

---

# There's a Better Architecture

**imajin** is a presence platform. Instead of logging what your people do into software they don't own, you give each person a sovereign presence — a Jin — that learns from their work, holds their context, and answers queries on their behalf.

When someone asks *where are we on the Henderson account*, they're not waiting for a reply. They're querying the presence of the person who knows. The answer comes back attributed, accurate, and routed through the actual human relationship — not a CRM field last updated six months ago.

Your people are still in the loop. The presence composes. The human verifies and sends. Nobody gets replaced. The cognitive overhead of constant context-sharing does.

# The Graph Is the Gold

Most organizations can't answer a simple question: *where does our wisdom actually live?*

Not on the org chart. Not in the job titles. In the people. In the specific humans who've built the relationships, solved the problems, learned the things. The ones who if they walked out the door tomorrow you'd be in serious trouble — and you know who they are, but only because you've been around long enough to notice.

The imajin trust graph makes that visible. Not through surveillance. Through use. Every query that flows through the network, every presence that answers, every attribution that settles — it builds a living map of where knowledge genuinely lives in your organization. Not where it's supposed to live according to the hierarchy. Where it actually lives.

The graph can also organize hierarchically, the way organizations need to operate. Queries can flow upward through the structure when that's appropriate. But the default is lateral — through the trust relationships where real answers live — because that's how work actually gets done, and we're not going to pretend otherwise.

A contact someone creates in the network is attributed to the person who built that relationship. Not to the company's database. The relationship belongs to the human who made it. The organization benefits from access to it. That's a fundamentally different model from every CRM ever built — and it's the only model that reflects how relationships actually work.
