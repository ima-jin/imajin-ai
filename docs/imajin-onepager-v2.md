## THE PROBLEM

Every time someone taps their card, 3% disappears to Visa, Stripe, and the banks. Every business begs customers to leave reviews on platforms they don't control — platforms that profit from the conflict between businesses and reviewers. Your 4.2 stars on Yelp aren't yours. They're Yelp's. Your Airbnb rating vanishes if you switch platforms. Your Uber driver's 5,000 rides mean nothing on Lyft.

Reputation is locked inside platforms that hold it hostage. Payments flow through middlemen who extract value from every transaction. Identity is rented, not owned.

The pieces to fix this have existed for years — in academic papers, in protocol specs, in fragments across dozens of projects. Nobody brought them together.

## THE INSIGHT

We identified five components that, when connected, make extraction structurally impossible:

1. **Identity** — you are a real, verifiable entity (not an account on someone else's platform)
2. **Payments** — money moves directly between parties (not through a chain of middlemen)
3. **Attribution** — every transaction records who was involved and how
4. **Reputation** — both sides credential each other automatically from real transactions
5. **Trust graph** — the network itself weighs those credentials based on history and relationships

Each exists in isolation somewhere. Identity companies build identity. Payment companies process payments. Review platforms collect opinions. We connected them into a single loop where a real payment produces real attribution that generates real reputation that feeds a real trust network.

The settlement is the anchor. Everything else is a layer of meaning on top of it.

## THE PHILOSOPHY

Every line of code is open source. Every service is self-hostable. Infrastructure is provided by local hosts all over the world — a federated network of sovereign nodes, each owned by the person running it. The protocol takes a percentage on every transaction. That's the business model. There's nothing central to shut down.

## THE GO-TO-MARKET

**For local businesses:** A QR code on the window. Customers scan, join with an email, and every transaction after that builds verified reputation for both sides. Fees drop from 3% per transaction to a one-time on-ramp cost — once money is in the network, peer-to-peer settlement is a ledger entry, not a card swipe.

**The pitch to the coffee shop:** "You keep more of your money. Your reviews are real. And you own them."

This applies to every industry with a middleman holding reputation hostage: hospitality, rideshare, freelancing, professional services, education. Same stack underneath. Same QR code. Same escape from platform lock-in.
