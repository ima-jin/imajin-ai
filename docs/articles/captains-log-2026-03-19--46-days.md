# Day 46

Hey everyone — stepping out of the essay format because the feedback I keep getting is: *I have no idea what you're building, what it's for, or how I fit into it.* Fair. Let me just tell you directly.

On February 1st I connected an AI to one of my lights as a presence. On February 17th I started building in earnest. 46 days later — 30 of them serious coding — I have a working platform. Built from scratch, from first principles, with the human at the center of every decision.

First — what do you actually *do* here?

**You host an event.** Sell tickets. Everyone who buys one gets automatically added to a private lobby chat. The ticket is the trust relationship. No Eventbrite taking a cut, no Facebook event that Zuckerberg owns.

**You message your people.** End-to-end encrypted. In a space that belongs to you, not a platform that can read it, sell it, or disappear it.

**You tip someone directly.** No platform taking 30%. The money goes from you to them.

**You take or teach a course.** Enrollment, payments, progress tracking — it's just there.

**You share your work.** With an attribution chain attached, so you're credited when it travels and you get paid when it earns.

**You ask your community something.** And the answers live in your trust graph — not a feed that evaporates in 24 hours.

Coming soon: sell your stuff, post your news, get paid for your attention on your own terms, run your business, federate with other nodes.

Here's the thing underneath all of it. Every platform you use right now is extracting from you. Your attention, your data, your relationships — they're the product. The platform gets paid. You get access.

I built this the other way around. You're the vendor. Your presence, your knowledge, your attention — those belong to you, and when they create value, you're the one who gets paid.

46 days in, the platform is real and it works. But it only matters if people actually use it. I need people willing to show up, break things, ask for features, tell me what's missing, and start building their presence here.

That's what I need right now. If you want an invitation into the alpha, come find us.
