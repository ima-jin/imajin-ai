## Why This License Exists

Most open-source licenses protect the code. This one protects the *principle*.

The Software implements a protocol for making economic relationships visible — who made what, who got paid, and how value flows through a chain. That visibility is the point. A corporation that takes this software, runs it in production, and processes transactions without participating in the attribution network is using the tool while rejecting the thesis.

This license says: use it freely. Build on it. Modify it. Sell it. But if you're a large commercial entity, participate in the network. Make your transactions legible. Let the .fair manifests show where the money goes.

Individual developers, small businesses, researchers, educators — no conditions beyond the standard copyright notice. This isn't about restricting access. It's about ensuring that the organizations with the most economic power are also the ones contributing to the visibility of economic flows.

The alternative is what every other open-source project discovers: corporations extract unlimited value from free software and return nothing. We're not asking for money. We're asking for participation.

**The cost of commercial use isn't a fee. It's legibility.**
